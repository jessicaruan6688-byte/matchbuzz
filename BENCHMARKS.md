# MatchBuzz Benchmark Notes

This file lists the concrete reference products used to refine MatchBuzz, what each product is good at, and what was applied to this build.

## 1. Hupu

Link:

- https://www.hupu.com/
- https://m.hupu.com/home

What stands out:

- Real-time hot topics and high-frequency sports traffic
- Strong community segmentation by league, club, and topic zone
- Content flow that keeps users browsing and returning repeatedly

What MatchBuzz should learn:

- Traffic should not start from a generic homepage only. It should start from hot fixtures, hot topics, and repeatable community discussion.
- Sponsor inventory works better when attached to repeat-visit modules like trending, polls, and matchday discussion.

What is now reflected in MatchBuzz:

- More visible live discussion, support meter, and trending modules
- Homepage revenue section explicitly maps sponsor placements to matchday traffic
- Match detail page now shows a sponsor-inventory layer rather than stopping at data only

## 2. Taopiaopiao

Link:

- https://www.taopiaopiao.com/?tbpm=3

What stands out:

- Very clear browse-to-transaction path
- Rankings, hot items, and local venue discovery are all close to the purchase CTA
- The product does not hide the commercial layer; it makes buying the next obvious action

What MatchBuzz should learn:

- A sports traffic product should not stop at content engagement.
- Match pages should naturally lead into ticketing, watch-party reservation, local event packages, or merchandise conversion.

What is now reflected in MatchBuzz:

- Homepage revenue section now includes a visible ticketing and commerce funnel
- Match detail page now includes Taopiaopiao-style ticket and merch conversion modules
- Campaign detail page now includes revenue cards for ticket redirect, fan bundle, and sponsored package design

## 3. FotMob

Link:

- https://www.fotmob.com/matches
- https://www.fotmob.com/ckb/aboutUs/advertise

What stands out:

- Strong list-to-detail navigation for live football behavior
- Explicit advertising and World Cup activation logic
- Product stays user-first but still exposes high-value ad inventory

What MatchBuzz should learn:

- Matchday products need a repeated visit loop: fixtures -> detail -> stats -> back to fixtures
- World Cup or major-tournament ad packages should be visible, not just implied

What is now reflected in MatchBuzz:

- Real fixtures page and detail page with standings and related fixtures
- Homepage commercial section now explains premium matchday sponsor packages
- Product structure is less like a PPT and more like a repeatable football utility

## 4. Sofascore

Link:

- https://www.sofascore.com/football/livescore

What stands out:

- Deep football utility: live scores, statistics, league tables, fixtures, and team detail loops
- Strong retention driven by navigation depth, not just one landing page

What MatchBuzz should learn:

- A football product must have depth in data structure and return paths
- Utility pages create trust, and trust supports commercial conversion later

What is now reflected in MatchBuzz:

- Real fixtures list
- Match detail page with standings and surrounding fixtures
- Studio can preload a real fixture directly into the content workflow

## 5. ScorePlay

Link:

- https://www.scoreplay.io/
- https://www.scoreplay.io/products/creator

What stands out:

- AI-tagging and automated distribution to athletes and partners
- Sponsor and partner collaboration built into the workflow
- Sports organizations use it as media infrastructure, not just a content editor

What MatchBuzz should learn:

- Content generation is not enough. Distribution logic and partner delivery must be visible.
- Sponsors and partners should appear as first-class workflow outputs.

What is now reflected in MatchBuzz:

- Campaign detail page now has a clearer revenue layer and benchmark notes section
- Ops console, campaign detail, and export brief reinforce the workflow story
- The product is framed as an operator stack, not a generic chat demo

## 6. Greenfly

Link:

- https://www.greenfly.com/solutions/sports/

What stands out:

- Explicit sponsor activation and media-partner value
- Content collection, organization, and distribution are tied to fan engagement and revenue
- Strong positioning around short-form content for sports organizations

What MatchBuzz should learn:

- Revenue should be tied to sponsor activation, not only software seats
- Content workflows become stronger when they serve sponsors, broadcasters, and creators at once

What is now reflected in MatchBuzz:

- Homepage benchmark and revenue sections now mention partner and sponsor workflows more explicitly
- Campaign detail page now separates content output from revenue output
- Commercial model is framed as media layer + conversion layer + SaaS layer

## Short Summary

After the benchmark pass, MatchBuzz is no longer positioned as only:

- a content generator

It is now positioned as:

- a football traffic product with fixture depth
- a content workflow product for creators and operators
- a sponsor inventory and ticketing conversion surface
